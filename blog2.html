Rohan Doijode - Goa Trip

Day 1 Day 2 Day 3 Day 4

Day 1: Arrival at Palolem – The Calm Before the Chaos

We arrived at Madgaon station with the excitement of a cinematic road trip ahead. After picking up our rental car, we drove straight to Palolem Beach, much like Akash, Sameer, and Siddharth on their carefree Goa trip in *Dil Chahta Hai*. The beach was just a short walk from our hostel, and we enjoyed an Italian meal at Bridge Inn, embracing the quiet charm of the coast. The rest of the day was spent soaking in the coastal breeze and capturing those perfect snapshots.

Day 2: Kayaking at Cola Beach – Living in the Moment

The second day was all about adventure. After breakfast at Caesar's Café, we drove to Cola Beach and began our kayaking session, channeling the same carefree spirit as Sameer from *Dil Chahta Hai*. Paddling through the serene backwaters, it was a moment of calm and reflection. Our stay at the Portuguese-themed Fenacia Resort later in the day gave us a glimpse of Goa's rich colonial past. We ended the evening with a beautiful sunset at Cabo De Rama, creating memories that, much like Sid's art, would last forever.

Day 3: Cabo Da Rama Solitude & Benaulim Beach – Reflection and Revelry

I decided to start Day 3 solo, returning to Cabo Da Rama for a quiet morning of reflection—much like Siddharth seeking solace in the beauty of the ocean. The day shifted from calm to lively as we explored Benaulim Beach and enjoyed live music at Johncy's Café. As we strolled down the beach, the music echoed the vibes of Goa’s laid-back yet vibrant culture. The day ended with a fire-breathing show at Southern Deck, leaving us in awe of the raw energy and talent.

Day 4: Farewell to Goa – Bittersweet Goodbyes

The final day was a mix of emotions as we packed up and returned the rental car. Much like the friends parting ways at the end of *Dil Chahta Hai*, we left with promises to return someday. Goa, with its beauty and energy, left an indelible mark on us. As the train pulled away from the station, we couldn't help but hum the iconic tune, *“Dil Chahta Hai, kabhi na beetein chamkeele din...”*
